#+title: DuckDuckGo’s Quest to Prove Online Privacy Is Possible
- Source:  https://www.wired.com/story/duckduckgo-quest-prove-online-privacy-possible/
- Tags: [[id:335ce537-26fd-4235-ad89-7c800282dbf7][Privacy]]

- According to their market research just about all demographics want more privacy
  + 93% of Americans would switch to a company that prioritizes data privacy if given the option. ([[https://www.datocms-assets.com/16414/1597294492-transcenddataprivacyfeedbackloop-2020.pdf][Source]])
  + 57% of Americans would give up personalization for privacy ([[http://www.conference-board.org/press/data-privacy-survey-nov2020][Source]])
- Apple: App Tracking Transparency system
  + opt-in to being tracked by third parties: only a minority does

- People seem resigned to get no privacy
  + [[id:5a01ead7-13fa-4d62-86ea-8e9a015c7a75][Gabriel Weinberg]]: People want privacy, but they feel like it’s impossible to get, so our main challenge is to make the idea that you can get simple privacy protection credible.
  + Only way to compete with Google being not to compete on search results
